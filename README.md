#Early Warning Indicators of NASDAQ Asset Bubbles via Hybrid PSY-LSTM Models"  

Abstract of research work 

Financial bubbles—periods of unsustainable price growth followed by sharp crashes capable of triggering economic crises—pose a significant threat. With rapid advances in technology and artificial intelligence, financial markets have become increasingly dynamic and prone to overheating. The NASDAQ index, representing the technology sector, is particularly exposed to these risks, underscoring the need for robust early-warning systems based on market indicators. This study introduces a hybrid, forward-looking framework integrating the econometric PSY-test with deep learning classifiers, and employs Permutation Importance to identify the most critical macroeconomic, technical, and behavioral signals for forecasting bubble onset. Using monthly NASDAQ data from 1995 to 2024, the framework first establishes bubble-phase boundaries through the PSY-test, then trains a three-layer LSTM model on 33 macroeconomic, technical, and behavioral indicators to predict bubble onset at horizons of 3, 6, 9, and 12 months. The model achieves F1-scores of 91%, 93%, 90%, and 92% for these respective horizons, with M2 money-supply growth emerging as the sole consistent predictor across all leads. Our contribution is an interpretable model that detects NASDAQ bubbles in advance and clarifies which indicators drive each alert.

Key words: Early-warning indicators, Financial bubbles, Multi-horizon forecasting, Permutation importance, PSY-test
